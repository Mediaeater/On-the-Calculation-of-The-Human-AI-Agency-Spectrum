# About

Through our work at [narrative.new](https://narrative.new), I've spent a lot of time thinking about human agency in the age of AI — agency in process, in tools, in UX/UI, and at the LLM and system level.

The existing language fails us. "AI-generated" and "AI-assisted" flatten a spectrum into two bins. Guild contracts say "human-led" without defining it. Copyright law says "significant human control" without measuring it. Tool builders make design choices that shape authorship and nobody names what's happening.

This framework is an attempt to fix that. Six layers. A measurable threshold. A shared vocabulary that connects to the legal, professional, and cognitive standards already governing creative work.

We're sharing it now because the window matters. Contracts are being renegotiated. Regulations are taking effect. Studios, unions, creators, and tool builders are all making decisions that will set defaults for years. The scaffolding and language to talk precisely about where we are — and where we're headed — shouldn't be proprietary.
